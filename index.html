Cat Got Di Ham

Di notorious Reno Snatcher don strike again as local cat Gino continue im crime activities, dis time e run comot wit special meat for broad daylight.

Natalie (Age 8) | Mar 6, 2025
Cat Got Di Ham (prod. DJ Kokonutz)
Mantis
Grammy-winning artist Mantis don already release one song wey describe di heist.

Authorities bin hear about anoda bold-face tif for Bret and Natalie house on Tuesday afternoon wen di infamous serial food tif, wey law enforcement pipo dey call "Di Reno Snatcher," carry half pound of honey-glazed ham comot for wetin witnesses describe as "perfect crime wey dem execute well-well."

Di suspect, one two-year-old gray tabby wey dey operate wit di name "Gino," don dey implicated for many-many similar tifs across North Reno area, all of dem follow di same pattern: e dey strike anytime kitchen surface no get person wey dey watch am.

"I bin turn my back for less dan one minute," na so victim Natalie report, wey bin dey prepare sandwich for di time of di incident. "Dat na all di time e need to complete di operation. By di time I turn back, na only one paw print for di cutting board remain."

Co-victim Bret witness wetin happen afta. "E no even try to hide di evidence. Wen I waka enter, e bin dey under di dining table, dey look me straight for eye as di ham dey hang from im mouth. E be like e wan make we know say na im do am."

"Di stealth, precision, and opportunistic nature of dese tifs suggest highly evolved hunting strategy."

Dis latest incident add join Di Reno Snatcher long-long rap sheet. Since dem adopt am from Washoe County Animal Shelter last year, Gino don dey linked to many food items wey disappear including rotisserie chicken, salmon fillets, pepperoni, and one whole block of cheddar cheese.

Local veterinarian Dr. James Wilson don dey follow di case wit professional interest. "Wetin we dey see here na classic predatory behavior wey channel enter domestic settings," na so e explain during Gino recent check-up. "Di stealth, precision, and opportunistic nature of dese tifs suggest highly evolved hunting strategy."

Wen dem ask am about potential rehabilitation for di repeat offender, Dr. Wilson no too get hope. "Recidivism rates for dis kain behavior dey extremely high. Di best approach na preventative measures—secure food storage, constant vigilance, and make una no underestimate di perpetrator abilities."

Despite say dem don implement sophisticated security measures wey include motion detectors and childproof locks, Bret and Natalie house still dey fall victim to Di Reno Snatcher criminal enterprises.

"We don try everytin," Bret sigh. "E be like to dey live wit small Professor Moriarty. E dey study our defenses, identify weaknesses, and exploit dem wit terrifying efficiency."

Afta di ham heist, di perpetrator go im usual post-crime location—one sunny windowsill for di living room—where e spend several hours for wetin be like self-congratulatory grooming session.

Di investigation still dey continue, though local authorities acknowledge say prosecution no go likely happen because of di suspect adorable face and excellent attorney (imself).

Natalie na freelance writer and unwitting food supplier to Di Reno Snatcher. She dey live for Reno wit her husband Bret and dia criminal mastermind cat. Wen she no dey document felony food tifs, she dey enjoy hiking di Sierra Nevada and plotting elaborate revenge wey no dey ever materialize.
